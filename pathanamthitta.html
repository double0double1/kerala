Major Tourist Attractions of Pathanamthitta

Gavi

Gavi is an Eco-Tourism project of the Kerala Forest Development Corporation.It offers its visitors activities like trekking, wildlife watching, outdoor camping in specially built tents, and night safaris. Gavi is inside the Ranni reserve forest and is a part of the Periyar Tiger Reserve.

Sabarimala

It is the ‘Sacred Abode of Lord Ayyappa’ and one of the most important Hindu pilgrim centre in the country. The holy shrine is located amidst dense forests in the rugged terrains of Western Ghats which is inhabited by various wildlife species. The routes are famous for its scenic splendour and mythological value.

Aranmula

A little temple town in Pathanamthitta District, surrounded by undulating green hillocks and girdled by the holy river Pampa. The town is famous for the Aranmula Vallamkali(Boat Race) and the Aranmula Mirror,which is made from a mysterious blend of bronze and not found any where else in the world .

Trivia

The Pathanamthitta town is known as the 'Pilgrim Capital of Kerala'

Konni

A hill town, Konni is famous for timber trade and wild elephant training centre and is a charming town with lush meadows and hills stands on the bank of river Achakovil. Surrounded by clove and pepper plantations, Konni has a vast expanse of lush green forests and is the eco-tourism hub of Pathanamthitta district.

Adavi

A picturesque forest patch, 10 km from Konni on the banks of river Kallar, Adavi has nearly 5-km river frontage. The Adavi Eco-tourism project jointly launched by the departments of Forest and Tourism is part of the third phase of Konni Eco tourism project

Perunthenaruvi

A natural fall, down a rocky path into a ravine about 100 feet deep is the Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls. It is during monsoons that one can truly appreciate the roaring waters of Perunthenaruvi falls. The name of the place translates to ‘great honey stream’ and has turned into a famous picnic spot in the district.
