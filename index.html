Kerala

God's Own Country!

Kerala, fondly referred to as ‘God’s Own Country’, is located on the south-western tip of India and enjoys unique geographical features that have made it a paradise on Earth. The western side of this land is lined by nearly 600KM of Arabian Sea shoreline and the inlands are filled with lush-green forests and mountain slopes of Western-ghats.

Kerala was selected by the National Geographic Traveller as one of the 50 destinations of a lifetime and one of the thirteen paradises in the world.

Major Cities


Thiruvananthapuram

Capital city of Kerala, distinguished by its British colonial architecture and many art galleries.
Thiruvananthapuram has historically been a cultural hub in South India due to the development of arts, architecture and liberal customs by the rulers of erstwhile Trivandrum.

Kochi

Called the "Queen of the Arabian Sea", Kochi was an important spice trading centre on the west coast of India from the 14th century onward, and maintained a trade network with Arab merchants from the pre-Islamic era. Kochi is also the financial, commercial and industrial capital of the Kerala state.

Thrissur

Known by its historical name Thrissivaperur, Thrissur was a point of contact for the Assyrians, Greeks, Persians, Arabs, Romans, Portuguese, Dutch and English. Thrissur is also known as the Cultural Capital of Kerala because of its cultural, spiritual and religious leanings throughout history.

Kozhikode

Also known as Calicut, Kozhikode is a city along the Malabar Coast. In history, Kozhikode is dubbed the City of Spices for its role as the major trading point for Indian spices. The central Kozhikode Beach, overlooked by an old lighthouse, is a popular spot for watching the sunset.
