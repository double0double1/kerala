Major Attractions of Thiruvananthapuram

Kovalam Beach

The most famous of Kerala, Kovalam beach has got a a long coastline, a lighthouse on the shore,and clear waters. One can admire the beautiful sunset and also indulge into activities like sunbathing, snorkelling, catamaran rides and cycling on the beach.

Pathmanabhaswamy Temple

The temple is situated inside the heart of Trivandrum at East Fort and is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. It is also known for being one of the 108 abodes of Lord Vishnu the temple is embellished by murals and stone carvings and the deity in the eternal sleep posture.

Agasthyakoodam

Agasthyakoodam gets its name from The Hindu sage Agastya, one of the Saptrishis of the Hindu Puranas. The hill-station is famous for rare herbs that are of high medical value that are found on the slopes of the hill. Diverse in its flora and fauna it also is a delight for the bird-watchers.

Etymology

The city takes its name from Malayalam word thiru-anantha-puram.

Meaning, The City of Lord Ananta

Veli Tourist Village

Veli Tourist village is popular amongst the weekends due to its magnificent sites of the Veli Lake meeting the Arabian Sea. The village is connected with the Veli beach by a floating wooden bridge to be crossed on foot. Boating facilities are available in the village to enjoy a panoramic view of the place.

Shanghumukham Beach

Away from the crowd of the city on the western side of Trivandrum lies the Shanghumukham Beach. The silvery white sand, a never ending coastline and tranquil waters make it serene. With eating kiosks on the beach it is a perfect way to spend quiet evenings by the Sea.

Varkala Beach

Varkala Beach is quiet but it still is one of the most popular amongst the travellers. The beach is famous for being under the cliff and offers a breath taking view of the Arabian Sea. The beach also has a quiet beach called the Black Beach which is famous for its black sands.
